Our First Canada Day!

This is our First Canada Day in Vancouver. It celebrates the anniversary, which united the three separate colonies of the Povince of Canada, Nova Scotia, and New Brunswick into a single Dominion within the British Empire called Canada.

Gastown Steam Clock

The Steam Clock was built in 1977 by Raymond Saunders and Doug Smith. The clock was built as a monumnet and placed over a steam grate. Every quarter hour, the Steam Clock whistles and shoots steam from its five whistles.

My next door neighbors!

I live in the red building right next to the Fire House in Gastown. You can hear the sirens all day long. The firefighters are very nice and always say good morning when I go to College.

Fire Hall No 1!

This is the first Fire Hall in Vancouver and possibly the first Fire Hall in North America. It is no longer used as a Fire Hall, it was converted into the Fire Hall Arts Center in the 1970s.

Missy's First Bath!

This is Missy's first bath in Canada! She didn't really like the bath as she is used to showers. But we are in a new country and this is our house now, so she really needs to get used to it.

Vancouver War Memorial

The Victory Square Cenotaph was unveiled on 27 April 1924. The site of the Cenotaph is significant because it was at tables at the foot of the old courthouse steps where men signed up for the World War I. The courthouse was demolished.

Holy Rosary Cathedral

This is a Roman Catholic Cathedral located on Dunsmuir street. The construction began on July 16, 1899 and it opened on December 8, 1900. It is listed on the Vancouver Heritage Register and it is a legally protected building.

Christmas Tree in Robson Square

This is a huge Christmas tree! Although many of you might think that this tree is in Itu, it is actually on Robson Square in Vancouver, Canada.
